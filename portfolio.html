愛，是讓缺席的人永遠在場──《葬送的芙莉蓮》
Love from the deceased never fades away──Frieren: Beyond Journey's End

原文網址：https://okapi.books.com.tw/article/17348

URL to original article: https://okapi.books.com.tw/article/17348

初譯日期：2024.11.23 / 一校日期：2026.06.03

我發現將作品與事件當魔術方塊看，每次看就會有不同發現。

I realize that if I treat a workpiece and its storyline like a magic cube, I discover something different every time.

比方從人心來看那些回憶的流變，或從事件中鑽個小孔來看人性的切面，這對我來講都是生之樂趣，它不見得會接近真相，但比較接近我人生想追的真理。

For example, let's think about the evolution of memories from the perspective of a human's mind, or peek through the composition of an event to glimpse at many facets of human nature, which for me, entails the joy of life. The piercing through does not necessarily take us closer to the facts of an event, yet we take one step forward to the true wisdom of life.

如果電影大師塔可夫斯基說當個「合格的讀者」是重要的，那我們何妨一路當個找答案的人，在找答案的過程中，它就是你自己的故事了。

Audrey Tarkovsky, film master, once said it's important that we be a “qualified reader.” Why should we be seeking a real answer when we can already write our own story in search of that answer?

《葬送的芙莉蓮》的哲思核心直逼井上雄彥的《浪人劍客》：人生成功達標後，是否是一路競逐，甚至感到無人同行的空虛孤獨，還是大夢初醒般走出群體供奉的「理想與成功魔咒」？一開始就是戰勝魔族的強者歸來，登上了他們人生的榮耀時刻，當時都那麼年輕的他們，之後要面對的就是「平凡的漫長」與他人的「遺忘」。這是一個很反高潮的英雄影集，卻真實地在不與他人同的狀態中找回了自我，包括長生不老的精靈主角──芙莉蓮，一路承受認識的人陸續死去後，所能體會的「缺席」如同「在場」的人生真理。

The philosophical idea of “Frieren: Beyond Journey's End” can be compared to that of Inoue Takehiko's “Vagabond,” which prompts us to think about whether life continues to be a competition after we have succeeded in doing what we want, even if we are alone with no one by our side, or will we be awakened and enlightened, free from “the curse in pursuit of an ideal world and success”? From the very beginning of the story, the characters have conquered the demons, returned in victory, and enjoyed a glorious moment of their life. At that time, they were so young, yet they must soon be faced with an “ordinary, prolonged life” and “being forgotten” by all the other people. This story doesn't sound like a heroic film series dripping with climbing plots, but it faithfully depicts the finding of self once we no longer wish to be the same with others. Our main character, the elven Fieren, is no exception. She has been suffering from the pain of losing someone she knows all along, and has a heartfelt feeling of the wisdom of life telling us how one's “absence” is actually their “presence” to us.

以她不老生命的襯托，所有悲歡離合都是轉瞬間的事，有如花開與落的必然。故事的第一集在慶祝勝利的狂歡中，本是英雄的遠征隊卻是最感寂寥的，一方面離別在即，二方面他們所經歷的也讓他們永遠不同於眾人，卡在平靜與騷動之間，英雄不易的是自處，更難的是接受自己緩慢的「落幕」。然而，渺小的人類有不可逆的宿命，但人類在她沒有人生盡頭的流浪旅程中，卻交給她了數個寶物，都是來自於人類短暫的「不朽」── 來自渺小人類雖死猶生的愛與惦念。

Because of her never-aging life, all the sweet and bitter moments are merely something of a fleeting moment, as we know a blossoming flower always withers. In the first episode of the story in which a celebration of the victory is depicted, the expedition crew, who were the most looked upon, turned out to be the most melancholic. On one hand, the parting is inevitable; on the other, what they have experienced makes them forever uniquely different from others. Torn between a peaceful, serenic ever-after and uproaring excitement that might otherwise follow, heroes learned the hard lesson of being at ease with themselves. What's even more difficult is to accept the fact that “the curtain always falls” and that they are the ones to pull the curtain. Human beings are so tiny and can't escape the unchangeable fate of decease. Yet, they have given the elf tons of treasures—in her never-ending life and wandering journey and humans' short-lived life and fleeing “eternity”—Golden Fleeces wrapped with humans’ love and lingering feeling of missing someone as if they were still alive.

What Trump 2.0 would mean for the US and the world
川普2.0對美國及全世界來說意味著什麼

原文網址：https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/editorials/archives/2024/11/11/2003826697

URL to original article: https://www.taipeitimes.com/News/editorials/archives/2024/11/11/2003826697

初譯日期：2024.11.23 / 一校日期：2026.03.26
Date of initial translation: 2024.11.23 / Date of first revision：2026.03.26

你準備好面對美國準總統唐納・川普的狂妄不羈了嗎？你可能認為這位前任和即將再任的美國總統是自由狂放的代名詞，絕對沒有其他可能的原因，全是源於他對萬事萬物厚顏無恥的態度。是的，你的感覺沒有錯。

Are you ready for US president-elect Donald Trump unbound? You might have thought the former and future president was already pretty unrestrained, not least because Trump has never shown anything but brazen disrespect for boundaries or limits of any kind, and you would be right.

然而，這位藝人出身，成功競逐總統的人物——他不僅如此，更將兩個角色融為一體——正如他的口頭禪：「你見識到的還太少。」

However, as an earlier entertainer turned president — and Trump combines the two roles — liked to say: “You ain’t seen nothing yet.”

那是因為此位第47任美國總統將入主白宮總統辦公室，幾乎不再受到任何拘束。

That is because the 47th US president would enter the Oval Office free of almost all constraints.

他可能可以做到所有他曾承諾的事與所有他曾威脅要做的事，沒有人能阻檔他，也沒有什麼事能阻撓他所想做的一切。

He would be able to do all that he promised and all that he threatened, with almost nothing and no one to stand in his way.

想了解他為何能夠如此，要從川普週二的勝選事件開始談起，了解這件事情的本質(nature)為何。他並非如2016年在選舉人制度(electoral college)中，僅以幾分之差險勝，這次他大獲全勝。

To understand why, it pays to start with the nature of the win he secured on Tuesday. He did not eke out a narrow victory on points, as he did when he squeaked through the electoral college in 2016.

這是川普的奮力一擊，所有搖擺州選票他已一舉入袋，也贏得普選票，是20年來第一位達成此壯舉的共和黨員。

This was a knockout that has Trump on course to bag every one of the battleground states and to be the winner of the popular vote, the first Republican to pull off that feat in 20 years.

但是，這麼說仍低估這次選舉對川普來說，在本質上多麽地讓他徹底反轉局勢。川普不僅大大地贏得勝利，而且滿地開花：在50州中的48州都取得更多支持，橫跨各個鄉村、城市及郊區，也囊括各個族群，包括曾經穩為民主黨支持者的拉丁裔族群。

However, even that is to understate the transformational nature of this election. Trump won big and everywhere: gaining ground in 48 of the 50 states, in counties rural, urban and suburban, across almost every demographic, including groups such as Hispanic voters, who were once reliably Democratic.

柯林頓主政時前政治顧問道格・索斯尼克（Doug Sosnik）表示：「2024年的選舉揭示美國國內自1980年雷根總統勝選以後，政治光譜最大幅度右傾的現象。」

“The 2024 election marks the biggest shift to the right in our country since Ronald Reagan’s victory in 1980,” said Doug Sosnik, a former political adviser in Bill Clinton’s White House.

引領這波紅色浪潮的，是已造成民主世界許多政府政權翻轉的「反在任情緒（anti-incumbency mood）」，這番情緒亦可見於英國，且不難理解。

What drove that red wave was the same anti-incumbency mood that has toppled governments all over the democratic world, including in the UK, and it is not too hard to explain.

美國人民仍持續感受到新冠肺炎之後，通貨膨脹所帶來的餘悸。只要和支持川普的選民說上幾句話，我上禮拜試了好幾次，話題總是以飛快的速度轉移到高油價及難以負擔的食品雜物帳單。

Americans are still feeling the hangover of the inflation shock that followed the COVID-19 pandemic. Any conversation with a Trump voter, and I had many last week, would rapidly turn to high gas prices and unsustainable grocery bills.

在這樣的氛圍下，人民心中有著一股衝動，想做的無疑是把在位者趕下台。讓對移民的恐懼吞噬人民，再指控民主黨盡是沿岸地區推崇自由主義的菁英份子，受制於激進進步派，且無法觸及一般民眾——這兩個情緒都被川普巧妙地煽動——他端上桌的就是一場輾壓式的勝利。

In that climate, the impulse is to kick out the party in charge. Throw in fear of migrants and the accusation that Democrats are the party of the liberal coastal elites, in thrall to the progressive fringes and out of touch with ordinary people — both sentiments expertly inflamed by Trump — and you have the ingredients for a crushing defeat.

最終結果是川普不僅將掌控白宮，也即將要控制美國參議院，眾議院可能也難以倖免。我們必須承認，八年前川普上任時，共和黨在國會山莊就已擁有多數席位，現今亦如此，但情況稍有不同。以往，共和黨國會議員中，至少存在幾位對川普抱持懷疑態度的溫和派黨員，隨時都準備好反抗/挑戰川普。現在沒有。川普的「讓美國再次偉大(Make America Great Again, MAGA)」黨已所向無敵。

The result is that Trump would have control not only of the White House, but also the Senate and most likely the House of Representatives as well. Admittedly, Republicans had majorities on Capitol Hill when Trump took office eight years ago too, but here is the difference. Back then, there were at least a few moderate, Trump-skeptic Republicans in Congress ready to defy the president. Not now. Trump’s hold on what has become the Make America Great Again (MAGA) party is total.

幾乎找不到像已故的美國參議員約翰・馬侃(John MaCains)這樣的人物了，這次選舉後根本沒有人能對川普比倒讚了。可以肯定的是沒有人足以對川普造成麻煩。他想要的，他都能得到。

There are next to no late US senator John McCains to give Trump the thumbs-down this time, certainly not enough to cause him trouble. What he wants, he would get.

這意味著，他可以提名任何他喜歡的人到所有重要的職位上，他知道他在國會的好好先生都會對他頻頻點頭表示同意。上次，他能感受到應該要選擇有責任感的共和黨員籌組內閣的壓力，或是應該要找尋最合適的人選擔任政府部門首長，這些官員繼而可以削弱或推翻他那些比較瘋狂的計畫。

This means he can nominate whoever he likes to all the key posts, knowing his yes-men in the Senate would give him the confirming nod. Last time, he felt pressure to appoint responsible Republicans to his Cabinet or to head federal agencies, officials who then went on to dilute or even thwart his wilder schemes.

這次，他可以讓自己被真正的擁護者擁戴，包括那些川普在造勢時已矢口否認參與其中，但已臭名遠播的2025專案計畫(Project 2025)提倡者。他想要的話，現在也可以實現他們的訴求，以確保那些堅信MAGA的支持者在美國政府體系掌控全局。

This time he can surround himself with true believers, including the apostles of the notorious Project 2025 plan that Trump disavowed during the campaign, but which he is now free to implement — thereby ensuring a full-spectrum takeover by MAGA loyalists of the machinery of the US government.

要牽制川普，仰賴美國最高法院不是個好主意。美國最高法院席位目前由六比三佔多數的右翼成員組成，這當然也是川普現象使然。在一份七月發出的裁決中，最高法院賦予美國總統對其所為的公務行為免於司法審查的權力，形同開出一張川普垂涎已久的空白支票。

It is no good looking to the US Supreme Court to act as a restraining hand. Thanks to Trump, that bench now has a six-to-three right-wing majority, and it has already issued the blank check he craved. In a July ruling, the court granted US presidents sweeping immunity for their official acts.

原本川普揮之不去的法律威脅將煙消雲散。他會很高興地發現，對他發動的多起刑事案件預計會暫停審查，理由是總統在任內不能被起訴。

The threat of legal jeopardy that once hovered over Trump would melt away. To his delight, the multiple criminal cases against him are set to be suspended, on the principle that a sitting president cannot be indicted.

那麼，有什麼可以讓川普收斂的呢？不會是輸掉下一次選舉的恐懼：憲法已禁止川普繼續參選下任總統（但不保證川普不會挑戰這樣子的限制）。傳統媒體會盡己所能防範這一切發生，但如果川普的時代教會了我們什麼，那就是美國訊息傳遞的生態圈已徹底改變。

What, then, would be left to hold Trump in check? It would not be fear of losing the next election: He is constitutionally barred from running again (although you would not bet against him testing that limit too). The conventional media would do their best, but if the Trump era has shown us anything, it is that the information ecosystem of the US has changed utterly.

50年前，如果三大廣播公司以及一些東岸報章雜誌宣告某位總統是騙子，那位總統就完蛋了，如同美國前總統尼克森付出慘痛代價學到的教訓。現在，主流媒體可以披露對川普最不利的證據，卻可如蜻蜓點水般，不起任何波瀾。

Fifty years ago, if three broadcast networks and a couple of east coast newspapers declared the president a crook, that president was finished, as former US president Richard Nixon learned to his cost. Now, the mainstream press can reveal the most damning evidence about Trump and it goes nowhere.

他的支持者若非從沒聽聞這些傳聞——那是因為他們的消息來源是親川普的電視台及社群媒體管道——就是他們聽到了，但斥為謊言。

His supporters either never hear those revelations — because they get their news from Trump-friendly TV and social media channels — or, if they do, they flatly dismiss them as lies.

我們已切切實實活在「另類事實(alternative facts)」的時代，這個時代給了川普極大的自由。他在任時可以做出令人髮指的事情，或是淪落為失格的總統，但數以百萬的美國人民卻永遠不會知道。

We truly live in the age of “alternative facts,” and that gives Trump enormous freedom. He could do heinous things in office, or simply fail as president, and tens of millions of Americans would never know about it.

我們很可能會看到川普放飛自我，這並非僅是民主一詞的反例而已，從很多方面來說都是很危險的事情，而這些危機都已攤在我們眼前。就舉一例來說，沒有什麼能阻擋這位新的舊總統履行一項他的競選承諾，讓反對引進疫苗、輕信陰謀論的小羅伯特・甘迺迪（Robert F. Kennedy Jr）掌管衛生部。若真如此，已有人警告小兒麻痺及麻疹將在美國捲土重來，為美國孩童帶來禍害。

The prospect of Trump unchecked is not merely an offense to abstract notions of democracy. It poses multiple dangers, all of them clear and present. To take just one, there is nothing to stop the old-new president making good on his promise to put the anti-vaccine fanatic and conspiracy theorist Robert F. Kennedy Jr in charge of public health. If that happens, there are already warnings that polio or measles could return to afflict American children.

或是想想看氣候的議題吧。川普已承諾挖鑿/開採北美最後一片原始荒地——阿拉斯加北極圈國家野生動物保護區——的石油。這是一處現任美國總統拜登決意要保護的地方，而川普卻要調派機具鑽井鑿油。這將進一步加速氣候危機的惡化，以麻州女巫鎮沙連(Salem, Massachusetts)來說，當地本月的氣溫已飆升至詭異的攝氏26度，這場危機由此顯而易見。

Or consider the climate. Trump has promised to extract oil from the last pristine wilderness in North America, Alaska’s Arctic national wildlife refuge. US President Joe Biden had moved to preserve it; Trump would send in the rigs. That would accelerate yet further the climate breakdown, a crisis that was unmistakable that day in Salem, where the temperature reached a weird 26°C this month.

川普現在可以拋下烏克蘭坐視不管，任由俄國總統普丁殘忍貪婪的黨羽糟蹋，任把北約組織視作一張廢紙——在川普明年1月20日宣誓就任當天即將成為事實。我們知道川普對北約共同防衛的核心原則嗤之以鼻，然而失去這一原則，聯盟將土崩瓦解。可是沒有人能幫他按下暫停鍵。

Trump is now free to abandon Ukraine to Russian President Vladimir Putin’s wolves, free to make NATO a dead letter — which it would be the day Trump is sworn in on Jan. 20. We know that Trump has contempt for NATO’s core principle of mutual defense. Without that, the alliance falls apart. Yet there is no one to stop him.

最後，這項任務將落在民主黨身上，但他們很快地將無法繼續在華府擁有法定權力。因為他們將無法再發起國會調查案或是召開官方聽證會，我曾諮詢一位資深的專業人士，問他有什麼可行的方法能壓制、甚而審問川普的所做所為，他的答覆是：「他們（民主黨）可以開個記者會。」

Ultimately that task would fall to the Democrats. Except they would soon wield no formal power in Washington. I asked one seasoned hand what practical tools the party had to restrain or even scrutinize Trump, given that they would soon lose their current ability to launch congressional investigations and convene official hearings. The answer: “They can hold press conferences.”

目前，民主黨人士把箭靶對內，不斷互相指責，不同派系之間將矛頭指向對方，討伐失去這場選戰的不是。這樣的過程無可避免會發生，但互相指責的時間越長/拖得越久，對川普來說更有利，因為很快地他將掌權麾舵，剛好這些人也分身乏術，無暇監督他。

For now, Democrats are turned inward, engaged in a round of recriminations as competing factions blame each other for the loss. That process is inevitable, but the longer it goes on the more it helps Trump, by removing one more check on the power he would soon wield.

我們知道川普想要執政的雄心壯志，因為他已經說了他想要執政。他告訴訪問他的福斯(Fox News)記者說，他「從執政第一天」就會是個獨裁者。我們知道他仰慕的領導人物是誰，從他對普丁及中共國家主席習近平浮誇的讚揚中窺知一二。但我們可以大膽的說，儘管川普仍將持續抱著他的渴望，各方的監察和制衡將會限制他，所以他的渴望終究只是幻想。

We know how Trump wants to rule because he has said so, telling a Fox News interviewer he would be a dictator “on day one.” We know which leaders he admires because of the way he gushes over Putin and Chinese President Xi Jinping (習近平). The assumption had always been that these fantasies of his would remain just that, because of the institutional checks and balances that fetter a US president.

但回過頭來，明年1月20日川普再次宣誓就職的時候，這些約束力量看起來若不是不堪一擊，就是不復存在。川普會變得狂妄不羈，可以恣意使壞。

However, when Trump renews his oath on Jan. 20, those restraints would look either badly frayed or entirely absent. He would be Trump unbound, free to do his worst.

Forensic Scientist Dr. Henry Lee on Observation: “Make the Impossible Possible”

課堂名稱：新聞寫作 - 頭條新聞

Course title: Journalistic Writing - Breaking News

完稿日期：2015.05.07
Date of completion：2015.05.07

Forensic scientist Dr. Henry Chang-Yu Lee (李昌鈺), who has solved more than 8,000 cases and served as the first Asian commissioner in Connecticut, the U.S., gave a talk on the importance of observation to a crowd of 100 in the Social Science Information Center (SSIC) in National Chengchi University (NCCU) on March 18. “Cold cases aren't ’cold,' but the observation is lacking,” concluded Dr. Lee. Dr. Lee provided examples of his own observation when investigating cold cases. “With only a cigarette butt, you know the suspect comes from a low-income family.” “To shoot a bullet in a person's own mouth, he has to trigger the gun with his thumb, not the index finger,” said Dr. Lee speaking of the Suicide of Vince Foster, President Bill Clinton's White House Counsel and Hillary Clinton's friend.

As one of the events of NCCU Career Month, Dr. Lee's speech also included his life experiences prior to becoming a forensic scientist. “Except for my son, who aspired to become a dentist and succeeded, no one really knows what they will do when they are young,” recalled Dr. Lee—He chose Central Police University for its free tuition and living stipend, and worked 3 part-time jobs as a lab assistant, waiter, and Gungfu master when pursuing his M.D. and Ph. D in biochemistry in the U.S. Never had Dr. Lee expected himself to become the first Asian commissioner in Connecticut.

The audience consisted of NCCU students, professors, citizens, and students from Central Police University. Many brought Dr. Lee's latest book Make the Impossible Possible (化不可能為可能). In reaction to the eager interaction from the audience, Dr. Lee kept giving out police badges to whomever answered his questions. Although Dr. Lee visited NCCU in 2007, the limited seats of 100 for this speech were quickly taken during the sign-up.
